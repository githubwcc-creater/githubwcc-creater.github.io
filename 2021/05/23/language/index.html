LANGUAGE

I came to your country with dreams,

Like colorful bubbles, (simile)

Falling on cold hard pebbles. (rhyme)

I speak your language perfectly,

however,

The squinting of your eyes, the scornful tone and raising eyebrows, (consonance)

are icy water pouring on my head in December. (metaphor)

Rumors about me are spreading like wild tornados, (metaphor)

Tearing me to pieces: (personification)

As the glass fragments, (metaphor)

The dim color but reflects dazzling light.

Only because my dark skin reminds you of dark witches. (rhyme)

I try to explain in your language:

The darkness of my skin,

Does not mean the darkness of my heart;

The accent of my English,

Does not mean the stupidity of my culture. (refrain)

Standing still against all my soul, (alliteration)

indignant and sorrow.

The curtain of night devoured the earth little by little, (personification)

the curling up smoke suffuses in the air.

Even though the crimson blood is drained, (symbolism)

My skin is kin to apatite, (metaphor)

the darker the rarer;

My language is the albatross towering above the ocean, (metaphor)

Refusing to be erased.

Now I stand upright in the tossed bow of the flying boat,

go ahead,

though the spoondrift is seething.

I am the master of my language,

I am the navigation of my soul.
