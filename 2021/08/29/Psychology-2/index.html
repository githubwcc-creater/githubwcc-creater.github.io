Psychology 2

Have you ever seen parents telling their little ones, “Baby, this is a girl. Just look at her long hair and pink dress?” It seems that short hair has been a symbol of masculinity for so long that it subliminally plants stereotype in people’s minds. Long hair belongs to girls, short hair belongs to men like pink and blue. In fact, long hair on men does not mean that they should be seen as feminine; hairs styles, long or short are poor indicators of one’s masculinity or femininity. Moreover, historically, higher social status is distinguished by longer hair on man, and masculinity is discovered through individual’s ability to be themselves. Nonetheless, why and how this current stereotype comes out? The main reason is the promotion of media. Loads of people posts articles about the recommended hairstyle of men on some social media sites, such as Instagram. Writers usually describe the model with particular hairstyle exaggeratedly because their articles need to be attractive to win more views. They probably nudge the public to have the similar hairstyle for people may regard it as fashion. Whereas, most of the suggested are short hair, and hence result in reinforce people’s mindset (male shouldn’t have long hair). With the wide spread, this circumstance becomes worse and worse. In order to improve the problem, the writers can involve more types of hairstyles and tell the public it is important to be unique and remain themselves. Moreover, a better education may remove the bias. Students can comment the current situation more objectively since they know more well-rounded knowledge.
