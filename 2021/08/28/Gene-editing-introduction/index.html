Gene editing introduction

In 1866, Mendel discovered that the laws of genetics laid the foundation for the development of modern genetics. After more than 150 years of efforts of scientists, molecular biology technology with genes has been in-depth treatment as the core of all directions of human diseases. From 1972 to 2009, gene therapy theory was first proposed as a potential solution for the treatment of human genetic diseases, to being selected as one of the top ten scientific advances in Science magazine in 2009. At present, gene therapy programs mainly use viral vectors to deliver exogenous genes to treat diseases. Although progress has been made, uncertain factors will induce the body to produce an immune response to the vectors, so that gene therapy programs have always existed other potential risks of disease. The emergence of gene editing technology provides new ideas for humans to reduce these potential risks. This is the timeline of the development of various gene editing methods.
