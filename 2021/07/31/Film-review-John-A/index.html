Film review-John A

Compared to what I learned before, this movie exposes more minor players, such as Anna Brown and Galt who make crucial changes during the process of confederation. Take Anna as an example, the wife of George Brown, her existence subliminally plants the seed of open minded deep in his heart, resulting in Brown’s agreement of the grant coalition. In addition, in order to solve the key issues of political deadlock and railway, new laws need to be proposed to change the government structure. George Brown disapproves the idea of building railways as he thinks this method to achieve modernization is a theft of the public purse and corruption. Whereas, Macdonald promises Galt building railways and then he sides with Macdonald. Throughout that time, the government work extremely inefficient because of the double majority, e.g., they have arranged 50 votes to decide where the capital city of Canada should be. Overall, the main theme is sacrifice. Even though the politicians make judgements depending on their own parties’ belief, they all eventually compromise so that the very nation can survive under harsh circumstance. At the moment George Brown sitting considering whether join in the grant coalition and who will be the following leader of the new birth country, despite having being criticize Macdonald for such a long time in the newspaper, he still supposes Macdonald suits more to be the governor because of his charm. In other words, they all have the essential passion for the birth of a country, which reinforce my perspective as the unity is stronger than several parts more often than not and much more problems can be solved. After watching, I feel intoxicated when achieving the grant coalition. While Brown chooses to stand by Macdonald, sunlight streams through the window on to their faces and chairs driving the plot to its climax. Similarly, I both perceived an idea of unity from the film and Chinese history. For example, Qin Dynasty is one of the most important dynasty in Chinese history which was a unified dominant country developed by state of Qin in the late period of the Warring States. That conduct strengthened and formed better military protection for the previous China.
